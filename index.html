Places to visit in Darjeeling

Darjeeling Ropeway

Explore the senic beauty of the tea estates with Mt. Kanchenjunga in the background while enjoying the iconic Ropeway in Darjeeling situated near the Rangeet Valley Tea Estate.

Darjeeling Toy Train Ride

The Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, also known as Toy train is a narrow gauge railway built during the colonial era. This train runs from Darjeeling to New Jalpaiguri/Ghoom is a one of kind experience and is famous among tourists.

Red Panda

Darjeeling zoo is home to some of the rare animals found in the himalayan regions, Red Panda being one of the main attractions. Within the zoo you can find the HMI museum displaying instruments and gears used by Tenzing Norgay and others.

Peace Pagoda

Situated on the slopes of Jalapahar Road this famous Buddhist Stupa is one of the most famous tourist attractions of Darjeeling. Adjoining this stupa is The Japanese Temple, another famous attraction among tourists visiting Darjeeling.

Tiger Hill

It is the highest point in Darjeeling situated on top of the Senchal Wildlife Sanctuary and one of the most senic places in Darjeeling. Tourists flock this place specially at dawn to enjoy the breathtaking views of sunrise on Mt. Kanchenjunga.

Dali Monastery

It is one of the largest Buddhist monasteries in Darjeeling situated near Dali and is one of the most famous tourist destinations in Darjeeling.

Places to visit in North Sikkim

Gurudongmar Lake

Situated at an altitude of 5430 m, one of the highest lakes in the world. The lake offers marvelous views of the Kanchendzonga range and Mount Siniolchu, one of the tallest peaks in the region.

Mt. Katao

It is a seculeded high altitude spot at an altitude of approx 15000 ft and offers breathtaking views of snow covered mountains and natural vegetations. Tourists can enjoy adventure sports activities here specially during the winter season.

Yumthang Valley

Situated at an altitude of 3564 m, surrounded by snow capped mountains, it is one of the most scenic and pictursque places to visit in Sikkim. The valley transforms into a vibrant canvas adorned with colourful wild flowers during the spring season, hence also known as 'Valley of Flowers'.

Zero Point

It is situated at an altitude of approx 4664 m and marks the end of the motorable road. This place is renowned for breathtaking views of the landscape and snow capped Mountains specially the Mt. Kanchenjunga.

Places to visit in East Sikkim

Elephant Lake/Kupup Lake

A high altitude lake in Kupup region near the Indo-Tibetian Border. It gets its name due to its shape similar to an elephant. This place is famous among tourists due to its scenic views.

Lampokhari Lake Aritar

One of the oldest Lakes in Sikkim, a emerald boot shaped lake situated near Aritar. Along with mesmerizing scenic views this lake offers boating for tourists.

Sillery Gaon

It is a small mountain village situated near Kalimpong City and tourists travelling towards East Sikkim often start their journey from this place. This place is surrounded by scenic views of Pine forests and offers full range view of the Mt. Kanchenjunga.

Nathula Pass

It is high altitude mountain pass situated at the border between India and China. The centuries old silk route passes through this region before entering China. This place is famous among tourists for beautiful views of snow covered mountains and China border.

Tsongmo Lake

It si a high altitude glacial Lake in East Sikkim and holds spiritual significance for both Buddhists and Hindus. It is famous among tourists for yak rides and rope way rides which offres breathtaking view of the lake and surrrounding snow covered mountains.

Places to visit in East Sikkim

Kanchenjunga Waterfalls

Kanchenjunga Falls is a scenic natural wonder located near Pelling, Sikkim. Cascading from a height of approximately 100 feet, this spectacular waterfall is believed to originate from the glaciers of Mount Kanchenjunga.

Singshore Bridge

Singshore Bridge is the highest suspension bridge in Sikkim and the second highest in Asia, offering stunning views of the valley and mountains. You can also enjoy adventure activities like bunjee jumping here.

Rabdentse Ruins

Rabdentse was the second capital of the former Kingdom of Sikkim from 1670 to 1814. The capital city was destroyed by the invading Gurkha army and only the ruins of the palace and the chortens are seen here now. This place holds spiritual significance for Buddhists.

Sidkeong Tulku Bird Park

Located near Rabdentse Ruins, this bird park is home to several different species of birds. The area is encased with enormous nets like a huge cage and visitors can walk around the place and often experience close encounters with the birds.

Khecheolpalri Lake

This lake is situated admist a forest and is estimated to be around 3500 years old. The lake holds spiritual significance for both Buddhists and Hindus, and is believed to fulfill your wishes.

Pemayangtse Monastery

This is one of the oldest and religiously siginificant shrines in Sikkim. Being around 300 years old, it is a must visit place as it offers the reflection of the rich architecture and the unwavering faith of the Buddhist followers.

Skywalk

Built near the 137 feet tall statue of Chenrezig, this glass floored bridge connects with the stairs leading to the statue. It also offers stunning views of Mt. kanchenjunga and is popular destination among tourists visiting Pelling.

Dooars

Situated at the foothills of Himalaya, Dooars, also spelled Duars, gets its name from the word “doors” as it serves as a gateway to Northeast India, Bhutan, and the hill stations of North Bengal, including Darjeeling and Sikkim. The region is known for its diverse biodiversity, lush green forests, wildlife, and ethnic peoples and cultures. There are several significant forests in the area like Gorumara National Park, Jaldapara National Park, and Buxa Tiger Reserve which are home to some of the most exotic animals and birds like elephants, one horn Rhinos, bisons, leopards, black-headed orioles, hornbills and many more. The serene beauty of this region makes it an excellent destination for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts

Places of attraction in Meghalaya

Living root bridge

One of the most famous tourist attractions in Meghalaya are these living root bridges. These bridges are formed by the roots of rubber trees and takes decades to grow. Hikes through forests to reach these bridges are an adventure in itself.

Mawlynong village

Known as Asia's cleanest village and the warm hospitality of the villagers makes it a must visit for tourists. Adorned with flowering plants, decorative bamboo structures and spotless clean streets makes visitin this place a one of a kind experience.

Dawki

A small town on the banks of the Umngot river near the India Bangladesh Border. The waters of this river are crystal clear and so transparent that it makes boats appear to float on air. Tourists can enjoy a boat ride on this river.

Seven Sisters Waterfalls

The iconic view of the seven distinct streams of water flowing down the cliff is absolutely mesmerizing. One of the best places to visist during monsoon when the falls are at their full glory.

Mawsmai and Arwah caves

Surrounded by misty hills all around, walking through these massive caves are an adventure in itself. The massive stalagmite formations along with numerous fossils engraved on the cave walls makes exploring these caves a one of a kind experience.

Umiam Lake

Umiam Lake, often called Barapani, is a vast man-made reservoir surrounded by pine forests and hills. It's a popular spot for boating, kayaking, and enjoying peaceful sunsets just outside Shillong.

Nohkalikai Falls

It is said to be the tallest waterfall in India plunging from a height of approx 1100 feet. Surrounded by forests and cliffs, it offers some of the best scenic and breathtaking views in Meghalaya

Places of attraction in Spiti

Key Monastery

One of the largest and oldest monastery in Spiti Valley, located at a scenic hilltop near Kaza at an altitude of approx 4166 meters. It is surrounded by snow capped himalayan mountains and offers one of a kind serene experience.

Komic Village

Often credited as being the highest village in the world connected with a motorable road. It is also renowned for the ancient Komic monastery and the spectacular view of the landscape all around.

Hikkim village

It is another small mountain village surrounded by spectacilar mountain landscape. It is mostly famous for having the worlds highest active post office.

Chandrataal Lake

Situated at an altitude of appros 4300 meters, this serene cresent shaped lake lies in the shadows of towering mountains all around. It is not only famous for its breathtaking scenic beauty but also beleived to be sacred.

Dhankar Monastery

Situated on a cliff at an altitude of 3900 meters. it is believed to date back over a thousand years. This ancient Buddhist monastery was once the capital of the Spiti Valley kingdom.

Chitkul

Chitkul is a remote village in the Kinnaur district of Himachal Pradesh, situated by the Baspa river and one of the last villages near the Indo-Tibetian border. The scenic beauty of this place is next to amazing and is great location for campping.

Kalpa

Kalpa is a scenic village in the Kinnaur district of Himachal Pradesh, facing the spectacular views of the Kinner Kailash mountain range and the Shivling look alike rock formation.
